# Go-To-Market (GTM) Strategy

This document details the customer acquisition and viral distribution strategy for **SpendScope**.

## 🎯 Target Persona

The ideal user for SpendScope is a **CTO, VP of Engineering, or Technical Founder** at a seed to Series A startup (team size: 5–50 people). 

### Persona Characteristics:
- They are building AI-native or software-heavy products and have fully adopted tools like Cursor, Claude Pro, and OpenAI APIs.
- They are busy and do not track individual tool licensing. They notice their monthly credit card bill increasing but lack the time to audit subscriptions.
- They are budget-conscious but lack access to discounted pricing models.

---

## 🔍 Pre-Intent Search & Content Triggers

Users will encounter SpendScope right when they begin questioning their AI tool overhead.

### What they Google:
- *"Cursor vs GitHub Copilot pricing for teams"*
- *"How to reduce Anthropic API bill"*
- *"ChatGPT Team minimum seat requirement cost"*
- *"OpenAI prompt caching cost savings"*
- *"Startup discount for Cursor AI"*

### What they scroll past on feeds (X/LinkedIn):
- Screenshots comparing the pricing tiers of Cursor and Copilot.
- Threads explaining how prompt caching works and how to implement it to save 30-50% on API bills.

We will reach these users where they discuss development operations and startup economics:
1. **Communities:**

## 🚀 30-Day Zero-Budget Acquisition Plan

Our goal is to acquire the first **100 leads in 30 days** using targeted content and direct outreach:

### Week 1: Soft Launch & Cold DMs (Days 1–7)
- **Action:** Share 5 case-study style posts on X showing mock audits (e.g., *"We audited a 10-person dev team and saved them $4,000/yr by removing redundant Copilot licenses. Check your stack here..."*).
- **Direct Outreach:** Send cold DMs to 20 seed-stage founders offering to run a manual spend audit. This helps refine our messaging based on direct feedback.

### Week 2: Content Launch & Show HN (Days 8–15)
- **Action:** Submit to Hacker News as **"Show HN: SpendScope – Free AI Spend Auditor for Startups"**. Keep the submission text technical, open-source friendly, and focus on the math engine.
- **Blog Post:** Publish a detailed guide titled *"The Hidden Tax of AI Tools: Why Your Startups Are Overpaying by 30% for Subscriptions."* link to the tool as the CTA.

### Week 3: Reddit Distribution (Days 16–22)
- **Action:** Write an data-rich post for `r/startups` and `r/saas`. Share anonymous, aggregated data from our first 100 audits (e.g., *"We analyzed $25k in AI spend and found 65% of teams pay for empty seats due to plans minimums"*). Ensure the content is educational, placing the tool link at the bottom.

### Week 4: Referral Loop Activation (Days 23–30)
- **Action:** Pitch the tool directly to seed accelerators, incubator managers, and venture capital associates. Offer it as a free value-add tool they can share with their portfolio companies to extend their runway.

## ⚡ The Unfair Distribution Channel

Our unfair advantage lies in **Credex's primary business model**. Credex acts as a clearinghouse for companies offloading unused AI credits. 

When a startup pivots or scales down, Credex acquires their pre-paid API keys or SaaS coupons at a discount. We can use these discounted rates as a direct incentive: **"Audit your spend, and we will source the exact pre-negotiated credits you need to cover your remaining bill at 30% off."** This converts a standard calculator into an entry point for Credex's primary inventory.
