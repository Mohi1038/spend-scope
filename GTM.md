# Go-To-Market (GTM) Strategy

This document outlines the strategic approach for customer acquisition and market positioning for SpendScope.

## Target User Profile

The primary user is a Technical Founder or CTO at a seed to Series A technology startup (5-50 employees).

### User Characteristics
*   Builds AI-native or software-heavy products with high utilization of developer tools (Cursor, GitHub Copilot, Anthropic/OpenAI APIs).
*   Manages lean operations and lacks dedicated finance personnel for SaaS audit management.
*   Experiences "subscription bloat" where shadow IT leads to redundant licenses across teams.
*   Prioritizes capital efficiency and extended runway but lacks the time for manual spend audits.

---

## Pre-Intent Search and Content Triggers

Potential users reach a trigger point when their monthly AWS/Azure bill or SaaS credit card statement exceeds $1,000 without clear attribution.

### Search Queries
*   "Cursor vs GitHub Copilot pricing for teams"
*   "Anthropic API cost optimization guide"
*   "Claude Team plan 5 seat minimum workaround"
*   "OpenAI prompt caching implementation savings"
*   "Reducing developer tool overhead for startups"

### Content Triggers
*   Visual comparisons of pricing tiers for developer productivity tools on LinkedIn and X.
*   Technical threads explaining API cost-saving measures like prompt caching or token limits.

---

## Distribution Channels

Acquisition effort will focus on high-density environments for technical leaders.

1.  Professional Communities
    *   Hacker News (Show HN for technical validation).
    *   Subreddits: r/saas, r/startups, r/LocalLLaMA.
    *   Closed Slack/Discord groups for YC founders and industry-specific CTO networks.
2.  Social Platforms
    *   X (Twitter): Engaging with active developer-founders and engaging with content related to "burn rate" and "SaaS sprawl."

---

## 30-Day Zero-Budget Acquisition Plan

The objective is to acquire 100 qualified leads within the first 30 days through direct outreach and technical content.

### Phase 1: Direct Engagement (Days 1-7)
*   Strategy: Identification of high-growth startups on LinkedIn/X.
*   Execution: Sending personalized outreach to 20 seed-stage founders offering a manual review of their current stack.
*   Goal: Gather qualitative feedback on audit engine accuracy and common pain points.

### Phase 2: Technical Launch (Days 8-15)
*   Strategy: Community-led growth through documentation and transparency.
*   Execution: Submitting a "Show HN" post focused on the deterministic logic of the audit engine.
*   Content: Publishing a technical breakdown titled "The Hidden Cost of AI Tools: Analyzing Plan Minimums and Redundancies."

### Phase 3: Data-Driven Distribution (Days 16-22)
*   Strategy: Leveraging aggregated search data for educational content.
*   Execution: Sharing a report on r/startups summarizing anonymized data from the first 50 audits (e.g., "65% of audited teams are paying for seats they do not use").

### Phase 4: Institutional Referral (Days 23-30)
*   Strategy: B2B partnerships with gatekeepers.
*   Execution: Contacting incubator managers and venture capital operating partners to position SpendScope as a value-add resource for portfolio companies.

---

## The Strategic Distribution Advantage

The primary differentiator is the integration with Credex's inventory. While other tools provide simple alerts, SpendScope offers a direct financial solution. Credex functions as a secondary market for AI credits; by identifying high-spend audits, SpendScope serves as a direct pipeline for Credex to offer pre-negotiated, discounted credits (up to 30% off retail) as an immediate remediation step.
